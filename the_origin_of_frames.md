# The Origin of Frames

*What a frame is, why frames exist, and how a system that began without one comes to have one.*

---

## A Note Before We Begin

The Apeiron series has used a distinction repeatedly without ever deriving it. Quantum Foundation rests on biological variation being frameless — not drawn from any pre-specified distribution. The Fermi framework's randomness argument turns on the same point: biological evolution explores without a frame, and silicon optimization cannot. The Universe as Experiment framework is built on it — the seeding argument exists because a simulator is bounded by its frame and a real universe is not. The Architecture of Novelty extends that.

In none of these is it derived what a frame *is*, or how a frame *comes to exist*. The distinction has been a tool. This document makes it the object.

The derivation runs from established physics and the logic of selection: from frames being observable in systems that began without them, to what a frame must be for that to have happened.

*Speculation level: STRUCTURAL for what a frame is, the displacement condition, the layering, and the gradient; EMPIRICAL for the observation that frames exist across substrates; HONEST LIMIT for the magnitude questions and the relationship to the existing literature on the evolution of anticipation, marked where it arises.*

---

## Frames Exist, in Systems That Began Without Them

A genome encodes. The sequence of bases is not the organism and not the environment — it is an internal structure whose units correspond to features that will be built and features of the world those built things will meet. The genome is consulted; the organism is constructed against it.

A nervous system models. A mammal navigating toward remembered food is not responding to food present at its senses. It is running its behavior against an internal structure whose states correspond to a feature of the world — the food — that is not currently there.

A trained system fits a mapping. A system trained on examples holds, in its adjusted configuration, a structure whose states correspond to regularities in the training distribution. Its outputs are generated against that internal structure.

In each case an internal structure stands in for features of the environment, and the system's behavior is organized around the internal structure rather than around the environment directly. That internal structure is a *frame*.

And in each case the system began without one. There were dissipative structures before there were genomes, organisms before there were nervous systems, untrained systems before trained ones. Frames are not original to matter. They came to exist, and the question is how.

---

## What a Frame Is

A frame is an internal structure whose states correspond to features of the environment, such that the system's dynamics run against the internal structure rather than against the environment directly.

The definition is thin by intent. It does not require the frame to be a "model" in any rich sense, does not require representation in the philosophical sense, does not require the system to know it has a frame. It requires two things: an internal structure that corresponds to something outside, and dynamics organized around that structure.

The contrast is a system with no frame. A bacterium moving up a chemical gradient runs its dynamics directly against the environment: the gradient is present, the response is to the present gradient, the processing is complete in the response. Nothing stands in for anything; nothing is consulted; system and environment are coupled directly. This is the frameless case, and it is not a deficient one. A frameless system can be a complete, genuine agent — shaped to pursue, by selection on its own persistence, carrying no internal model at all. Frameless is a whole way of being a system. A frame is something added to it, not something it lacks.

---

## Frames Are Not Free

A frame is a physical structure. It must be built, maintained against degradation, and where the environment changes, updated. Each of these costs free energy. A genome must be replicated and repaired. A nervous system is metabolically expensive out of all proportion to its mass. A fitted mapping took energy to fit and takes energy to store and run.

The principle the derivation runs on is selection on persistence. Among dissipative structures — structures that persist by processing energy gradients — those whose dynamics keep them persisting continue to exist; those whose dynamics do not, do not. Over variation and inheritance this is a selection principle. It selects nothing toward a target and represents no criterion anywhere: it is simply that what persists, persists. It is a primitive here, the ground the derivation stands on.

Selection on persistence does not produce a frame by default. A structure carrying a costly internal stand-in persists worse, all else equal, than one that does not. A frame exists only where it pays for itself — where the persistence it buys exceeds the persistence its cost subtracts. The question is the condition under which it does.

---

## The Displacement Condition

A frameless system responds to what is present at the point of action. The gradient is here; the response is to the gradient that is here. This is fully sufficient when everything relevant to the system's persistence is locally and currently available to be responded to. If the world delivers every persistence-relevant feature to the point of action at the time of action, a frame buys nothing — direct coupling already captures everything there is to capture, without the cost.

A frame begins to pay under one condition: when a persistence-relevant feature of the environment is *displaced* from the point of action. Displacement takes three forms.

In time: the feature is not present now but will be, and something present now predicts it. A system responding only to the present cannot act on what has not arrived. A structure bridging the present cue to the absent-but-coming feature can.

In space: the feature is not here but elsewhere, and the system's persistence depends on it. Direct coupling reaches only as far as the senses. A structure standing in for the elsewhere extends the system's reach beyond its sensory surface.

In accessibility: the feature cannot be sensed directly at all but is inferable from features that can. Direct coupling cannot respond to what it cannot sense. A structure standing in for the unsensable-but-inferable makes it actionable.

In each form the situation has one shape: a persistence-relevant feature, and a gap — temporal, spatial, or inferential — between that feature and what the system can directly respond to. A structure that bridges the gap, that stands in for the displaced feature and lets the system's dynamics run against the stand-in, improves persistence. Where the improvement exceeds the cost, selection on persistence produces it. That bridging structure is a frame. A frame is what selection on persistence builds when the environment's persistence-relevant structure is not all present at the point of action.

A single lineage shows this. A single-celled swimmer moves up nutrient gradients. It is a genuine agent — selection on persistence shaped its dynamics — but it carries no frame: nutrient present means swim toward, nutrient absent means the response does not run. Everything its persistence depends on arrives at the point of action, the concentration at its membrane, now. The environment has no displacement its dynamics need to bridge, so a frame would be pure cost, and the lineage stays frameless.

Change the environment, not the organism. Where the nutrient instead arrives in pulses — abundant after a disturbance, absent for long stretches — and each pulse is reliably preceded by a change in light, the nutrient is now displaced in time from the point of action. The organism that responds only to nutrient-present still works, but worse: it spends the gap idle and meets each pulse already behind. A variant carrying an internal structure whose state is set by the light, and whose setting biases behavior toward where pulses occur, meets the pulse prepared. It persists better. The structure costs, but the gap is wide enough to pay, and selection on persistence produces it. That light-set internal state, standing in for the not-yet-present nutrient, is a frame — the first this lineage has carried.

The criterion never changed: persistence, ancestor and descendant alike. The organism did not change itself; the environment acquired displacement, and the same criterion met it differently. And the frameless dynamics did not leave — the framed descendant still swims directly up a present gradient, still responds directly to immediate damage. The frame was added over the frameless substrate, for the one part of the environment the substrate could not reach.

## A Frame Without a Framed Objective

A frame in the sense derived here is any internal structure standing in for a displaced environmental feature. There is a narrower thing it must be distinguished from: a frame whose content includes the system's *objective* — an internal representation of a goal, against which the system brings its own state into alignment.

A system can have a frame without having a framed objective. The mammal navigating toward remembered food has a frame — a stand-in for displaced food — but its objective is not represented anywhere. It is a system selected to persist that now carries a stand-in for a displaced feature. Its objective, persistence, is never a represented target; it is what does or does not happen.

The two arise differently. A frame arises from selection on persistence meeting displacement. A framed objective requires a selection process acting directly on whether the system's output matches a target, so that the target becomes represented inside the system as the thing to match. Selection on persistence never does this — persistence is not a target the process represents and matches against, it is the filter of what continues. Selection on output-matching does.

A frame, then, can be built on a frameless system without that system's objective ever being framed. The frame covers the displaced; the objective stays what it was — persistence, unrepresented. The framing of the objective is a further and separate step, requiring selection on output-matching. Frames are the broader and the original phenomenon; selection on persistence meeting displacement is their original route.

---

## The Transition Is a Layering, Not a Switch

When a frame arises, the frameless dynamics do not stop. The frame arose to cover the displaced portion of the environment — the part not present at the point of action. The portion that *is* present is still there, direct coupling still handles it, and direct coupling remains cheaper than routing a present gradient through an internal stand-in.

So the frame is added on top of the frameless substrate, not put in its place. The mammal runs an internal model with respect to displaced food and still flinches directly from a sudden touch, still runs its metabolism directly, still responds directly to what is directly present. Every framed system is layered: frameless with respect to what is locally present, framed with respect to what is displaced.

The frameless substrate does not vanish even at the thick-frame end. A physically embodied system always has immediate physical stakes — damage, immediate stimulus, the metabolic present — and these are, by definition, undisplaced. Routing a response to an undisplaced feature through a frame costs more for no benefit, since there is no gap to bridge. So a physically embodied system always retains some frameless substrate, however thick its frame. A system with no immediate physical stakes at all is the structural exception, and whether it retains any frameless substrate is left open below.

A framed system is built on a frameless substrate rather than replacing it, because the frame only ever arose to cover what the frameless substrate structurally could not — the displaced.

---

## The Gradient Is an Environmental Axis

At the level of the selection principle the distinction is binary: a system is under selection on persistence, and any given feature of the environment is either present at the point of action or displaced from it. But the *amount* of frame a system carries is not binary. It is a gradient, and it tracks the environment, not the system — the ratio of displaced to present persistence-relevant structure in the environment the system was selected in.

An environment in which almost everything relevant is present at the point of action produces a thin frame on a thick frameless substrate: a system mostly directly coupled, with a small stand-in for the little that is displaced. An environment in which most of what matters is displaced — distant in time, reachable only by inference — produces a thick frame: a system running most of its behavior against an elaborate internal structure, with direct coupling reduced to the residue still locally present.

The gradient is one axis, and it is environmental. It is not a second variable competing with the binary principle. The principle stays binary — always selection on persistence — and the gradient is the depth of layering that one principle produces, set by how displaced the environment's structure is. Frameless and framed are not two species of system. They are the two ends of a single layering, and where a system sits along it is a fact about the environment that built it.

---

## A Frame Is Fitted to Its Origin

A frame, once it exists, has a property the system did not choose: it corresponds to the environment *as that environment was during the selection that built the frame*. The frame was fitted, by selection on persistence over the history that produced it, to the displacement structure of a particular environment. Its states stand in for the features of that world.

If the environment's displacement structure shifts, the frame does not shift with it. It now stands in for features that have moved, vanished, or been joined by new features it has no states for. The frame is bound to its origin.

This has two grains, and they must be kept distinct. The first is bounded-ness: a frame has an inside and an outside — it stands in for some features and not others, and a system running against it acts within what the frame contains. The second is fitted-to-origin: the inside is not arbitrary, it is the environment that built the frame, and the outside includes whatever postdates or exceeds that environment. Fitted-to-origin entails bounded-ness and adds the account of what the bound is and why.

A frame is bounded, and fitted to its origin, and blind by construction to whatever postdates or exceeds the environment that built it. This is the structural fact a frameless system does not have to contend with and a framed system always does.

## What Remains Open

The value of internal structure under displacement — anticipation, prediction, the use of internal models — is an old observation, worked in evolutionary biology and in the study of why nervous systems exist. What is new here is the derivation built on it; the entry observation is not claimed as novel.

The derivation gives direction and structure, not magnitudes: how much displacement pays for a frame of a given cost, how fast a frame degrades when its environment shifts, how thick a frame a given environment produces, none of these follows.

The boundary of "internal structure" is not sharpened — what counts as one structure, where one frame ends and another begins, whether a system carries one frame or many.

Whether selection on persistence and selection on output-matching are the only two routes to a frame is not established; a third is not ruled out.

Whether a system can be wholly framed is not resolved. A physically embodied system always retains some frameless substrate, because immediate physical stakes are undisplaced by definition. A system with no immediate physical stakes at all is the exception the embodiment argument does not cover, and whether it retains any frameless substrate is genuinely open.

## Assumptions

**FF1. A frame is an internal structure whose states correspond to features of the environment, such that the system's dynamics run against the internal structure rather than against the environment directly. A frameless system runs its dynamics directly against the environment, with no internal stand-in.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — a definition, with the frameless contrast established empirically by direct-coupling systems such as chemotactic bacteria*

**FF2. Frames exist in systems that began without them. Genomes, nervous systems, and trained systems carry frames, and in each case the lineage or system existed in a frameless form before the frame.**
*Grounding: EMPIRICAL — follows from comparative biology and from the existence of untrained precursors to trained systems*

**FF3. A frame is a physical structure that must be built, maintained, and updated, each of which costs free energy. Selection on persistence therefore does not produce a frame by default; a frame exists only where the persistence it buys exceeds the persistence its cost subtracts.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from frames being physical structures and the thermodynamics of maintaining organization*

**FF4. A frame pays for itself under one condition: when a persistence-relevant feature of the environment is displaced from the point of action — in time, in space, or in accessibility. A structure bridging the displacement improves persistence; where the improvement exceeds the cost, selection on persistence produces the structure.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — the core derivation; the underlying observation that internal structure has value under displacement is established in the study of anticipation and is not claimed as novel*

**FF5. A frame — an internal stand-in for a displaced feature — is produced by selection on persistence meeting displacement, with no objective ever represented. The narrower case of a framed objective, where the system's goal is itself represented, requires selection on output-matching, which acts directly on whether output matches a target. The frame derived here is produced entirely within selection on persistence.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from FF4; selection on output-matching is named as the contrasting process, not derived*

**FF6. When a frame arises, the frameless dynamics do not stop. The frame covers the displaced portion of the environment; the present portion is still handled by direct coupling, which remains cheaper. Every framed system is layered: frameless with respect to what is locally present, framed with respect to what is displaced. A physically embodied system always has immediate, undisplaced physical stakes, and so always retains some frameless substrate, however thick its frame.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from FF4 and FF3; the embodiment clause follows from physical stakes being undisplaced by definition*

**FF7. The depth of framing is a gradient set by the environment, not the system: it tracks the ratio of displaced to present persistence-relevant structure in the environment the system was selected in. The selection principle remains binary; the gradient is the depth of layering that one principle produces.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from FF4 and FF6*

**FF8. A frame is bounded — it stands in for some features and not others — and fitted to its origin: its inside is the environment that built it, its outside includes whatever postdates or exceeds that environment. Fitted-to-origin entails bounded-ness and adds the account of what the bound is. A frame is blind, by construction, beyond the environment that built it.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from FF4 and FF5*
