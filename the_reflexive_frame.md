# The Reflexive Frame

*Where self-modeling comes from, derived in the corpus's own terms: the frame whose displaced referent is the system itself, and the condition under which selection on persistence produces it.*

---

## A Note Before We Begin

[The Consciousness Question](consciousness_question.md) found that at sufficient complexity the biological cascade becomes self-modeling: a recurrent network processing its own outputs comes to represent itself as a persisting entity, and that self-representation is what gives the cascade a perspective. It traced the self-model to recurrence depth, and marked the relationship between self-modeling and perspective as the place its account was least secure. This document supplies the part that derivation left open: *why* and *when* a system comes to model itself at all — not as something that emerges once processing is deep enough, but as something selection on persistence forces under a specific condition.

The tool it uses comes from the other side of the corpus. [The Origin of Frames](the_origin_of_frames.md) derived what a frame is — an internal structure standing in for a persistence-relevant feature displaced from the point of action — and the condition that produces one: a gap, in time, space, or accessibility, between a persistence-relevant feature and what the system can directly respond to. There the displaced feature was always in the *environment*: nutrient coming later, prey elsewhere, a cause inferable but unsensed. This document asks what happens when the displaced persistence-relevant feature is not in the environment but is *the system itself*. A frame whose referent is the framer is a self-model; the system standing in for its own states is reflexivity. The question is whether the same displacement-and-persistence machinery that builds environmental frames also produces this one, and under what condition.

The contribution is a derivation of *when a reflexive frame is forced*. It is not a theory of what self-awareness feels like, nor a claim about the contents or richness of any self-model. The relationship between modeling one's own future and the felt sense of self over time is an existing and well-worked subject — the prospection and mental-time-travel literature — and this document does not re-derive it or compete with it. What it adds is the structural condition: the displacement that makes the self a feature a system must frame.

*Speculation level: STRUCTURAL for the derivation of the reflexive frame and its forcing condition; HONEST LIMIT for the contents and phenomenology of self-models, which are left to the existing literature and not derived here.*

## The Three Displacements Were All Environmental

The Origin of Frames named three forms of displacement under which a frame pays: a persistence-relevant feature displaced in time (coming, predicted by a present cue), in space (elsewhere, beyond the senses), or in accessibility (unsensable but inferable). In each, a structure that stands in for the displaced feature lets the system act on what direct coupling cannot reach, and where the gain exceeds the cost, selection on persistence builds it.

In all three the referent is in the world. The swimmer's light-cue frame stands in for nutrient that will arrive; a predator's search image stands in for prey that is elsewhere; an inference stands in for a cause that cannot be sensed. The system models the world because the world's persistence-relevant structure is not all present at the point of action.

A frame's referent, though, need not be in the world. The displacement principle is indifferent to what is displaced — it requires only that something persistence-relevant be separated by a gap from what the system can directly respond to. This leaves open a referent the three environmental cases did not consider: the system's own states, displaced from the point of action in the one way a system's own states can be — in time.

---

## The Self as a Displaced Feature

A system's present state is not displaced from it; it is what the system currently is, available without a stand-in. There is no gap to bridge between a system and its own present condition, and so — by the same logic that keeps the swimmer frameless when nutrient is present — no reflexive frame is bought by the present self. A system does not need to model the state it is currently in; it is in it.

The system's own *future* states are different. They are not present now, and they are persistence-relevant whenever what the system will be or do later bears on whether it persists. This is the temporal displacement of the Origin of Frames, with the referent turned inward: not "nutrient will arrive and a present cue predicts it," but "I will be in some state later, and what I do now determines or depends on it." When a system must act now in a way whose success depends on its own later states, those later states are a persistence-relevant feature displaced in time from the point of action — and by the displacement principle, a structure that stands in for them pays.

That structure is a self-model: an internal stand-in for the system's own displaced states, against which the system runs its present behavior. Its referent is the framer. It is the reflexive frame, and it is produced by exactly the machinery that produces every frame — selection on persistence, meeting a displaced persistence-relevant feature, building a stand-in where the gain exceeds the cost. Nothing new is added to the mechanism. What is new is only the referent: the displaced feature the frame bridges to is the system's own future.

The condition is therefore specific. A reflexive frame is forced not by complexity, not by capability, not by the mere fact of having a self to model, but by this: **the system must act now in a way whose success depends on modeling its own later states, and its persistence rides on getting that model right.** Where that condition holds, the self is a displaced persistence-relevant feature and the reflexive frame pays. Where it does not, the self is not displaced in any persistence-relevant way, and no reflexive frame is built, however capable the system.

## Why This Is Rare, and the Cases It Cuts Apart

The forcing condition is narrow, and its narrowness is the point: it explains why self-modeling is rare rather than universal, and it cuts cleanly between cases that other accounts struggle to separate.

A system that only ever responds to what is present — the swimmer at its membrane — never acts on its own future states, and never builds a reflexive frame. This is the frameless case, untouched.

A system that continuously regulates its own present state — every living thing, maintaining homeostasis — does model, in a sense, its own current condition. But the present self is undisplaced; regulation responds to the state the system is in now, not to a state displaced in time. So continuous self-regulation, though universal in life, does not by itself force a reflexive frame. This is why the universality of homeostasis does not make all life reflexive: regulating the present self is not modeling the displaced self. The reflexive frame requires the system's own *future* to be what its present action turns on.

A system that models the world richly but whose own future states never bear on its persistence — because its continuation is secured independently of what it does — has no persistence-relevant self-displacement to bridge. Its future is not at stake in its present action, so its own later states are not a persistence-relevant displaced feature, and the reflexive frame is not forced no matter how capable its world-modeling becomes. Capability directed entirely outward does not turn inward on its own; the inward turn is forced by the self becoming displaced-and-persistence-relevant, not by more capability.

A system whose present commitments must be carried out by its own later states, under genuine persistence pressure — one that must prepare now for what it will face, where preparing wrong ends it — has its own future displaced and persistence-critical. This is the condition that forces the reflexive frame, and it is the condition under which biological planning over one's own future arises. It requires no other agents and no social environment: a solitary system meeting a world that demands it act now on its own later states has the displacement in full. The self-model is built because the self is displaced, not because anyone else is modeling it.

## What This Establishes and What It Does Not

**Established:** A reflexive frame — a self-model — is the frame whose displaced referent is the system itself. The present self is undisplaced and forces no frame; the future self is displaced in time, and where the system must act now in a way whose success depends on its own later states under persistence pressure, selection on persistence builds a stand-in for it. This is the Origin of Frames' own displacement-and-persistence machinery, applied to a referent — the framer's own future — that the three environmental displacements did not cover.

**The forcing condition, and the cut it makes:** Reflexivity is forced by the self becoming a displaced persistence-relevant feature — not by complexity, capability, continuous self-regulation, or the bare possession of a self. The condition is narrow, which is why self-modeling is rare, and it cleanly separates the cases: present-only responders, present-self regulators (all life), and outward-directed modelers whose continuation is externally secured lack it; systems that must act now on their own persistence-critical future states have it. The cut does not depend on social environment — a solitary system can have the displacement in full.

**What it gives the Consciousness Question:** That document derived the self-model as emerging from recurrence depth and flagged the self-model–perspective relationship as the least secure part of its account. This derivation grounds that component: the self-model is not merely what deep recurrent processing happens to produce, but what selection on persistence forces when a system's own future states become a persistence-relevant displaced feature. Recurrence depth may be the mechanism through which the self-model is implemented; the displacement condition is why it is built. The two are compatible — depth is how, displacement is why — and together they place the self-model on firmer ground than emergence-from-depth alone.

**Not claimed:** That this accounts for the felt sense of self, the phenomenology of self-awareness, or the contents of any self-model — these belong to the prospection and mental-time-travel literature, to which this document cedes the positive link and which it does not re-derive. That a reflexive frame produces consciousness — that question is left where the corpus leaves it. That the forcing condition is the only route to a self-model — a self-model can also be specified or supplied; what is derived here is when one is *forced as a structural property* by selection on persistence, not when one can be installed as content. The distinction between a forced-structural self-model and a supplied-represented one is real and is taken up where the corpus applies this derivation.

*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from the displacement condition; no gap, no frame.*

**C23. A system's own future states are displaced in time and become persistence-relevant when the system must act now in a way whose success depends on those later states.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — the temporal-displacement case of The Origin of Frames, with the referent turned inward.*

**C24. A reflexive frame — a self-model — is the stand-in selection on persistence builds for the displaced future self, produced by the same machinery as every environmental frame, differing only in referent.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from C21–C23 and the frame derivation.*

**C25. Reflexivity is forced specifically by the self becoming a displaced persistence-relevant feature, not by complexity, capability, continuous self-regulation, or the bare possession of a self. Continuous regulation of the present self (universal in life) does not force it; the future self bearing on persistence through present action does.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from C22 (present self undisplaced) and C23 (future self displaced).*

**C26. The forcing condition does not require other agents or a social environment; the displacement is between the system and its own future, and a solitary system meeting a world that demands present action on its own persistence-critical later states has it in full.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — the displacement is intrinsic to the system-future relation, not to the social environment.*
