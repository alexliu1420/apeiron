# The Origin of Agency

*Where pursuit comes from in dissipative structures — and what kind of agency is distinct from what.*

---

## A Note Before We Begin

Goal-directed behavior exists in dissipative structures long before anything that looks like a mind. Bacteria pursue chemical gradients. Plants orient toward light. Slime molds optimize nutrient networks. None of these have internal models, none reflect, none represent objectives to themselves — yet all are correctly described from outside as pursuing. The question this document attempts: how does pursuit arise from physics alone, in structures that have nothing to pursue with?

The Origin of Frames derived what a frame is — an internal structure standing in for a displaced environmental feature — and the principle of selection on persistence from which frames arise. This document uses both. It takes selection on persistence and asks what it produces before any frame exists: agency in a structure that has no internal model at all. The result is a kind of agency distinct from the kind a system has once it carries a frame.

*Speculation level: EMPIRICAL for the thermodynamics and the selection logic; STRUCTURAL for the derivation of frameless agency and its distinction from framed agency; HONEST LIMIT for whether engineered systems develop frameless agency through training-like processes rather than through the selection biology underwent.*

---

## The Observation

A river dissipates gravitational potential without pursuing anything. A hurricane maintains its structure by processing temperature gradients without pursuing anything. A flame consumes fuel without pursuing anything. All are dissipative structures — local order maintained at the cost of faster global entropy increase. None act in ways correctly described as goal-directed.

A bacterium dissipates chemical gradients while moving up them. A plant dissipates light while orienting toward it. A slime mold optimizes nutrient-collection networks. All are dissipative structures. All also act in ways correctly described as pursuing — moving toward conditions, away from others, in patterns that reliably correlate with their continued existence.

The transition between these — from dissipative structures that don't pursue to dissipative structures that do — is the question. What structural property does the second class have that the first does not, and how did that property arise from physics alone, without any prior thing recognizable as agency to bootstrap from?

---

## The Derivation

**Dissipative adaptation.** Matter driven by external energy gradients tends to self-organize into structures that dissipate those gradients more efficiently (England, 2013, 2015). This is established and provides the thermodynamic basis for complex structure formation. Dissipation does not require inheritance. Hurricanes form and reform without inheriting properties from prior hurricanes.

**Inheritance.** A subset of dissipative structures produces additional dissipative structures whose configurations are related to their own. Cellular reproduction is the established case. The broader class of inheriting dissipative structures is less precisely characterized but cellular inheritance is sufficient for the derivation.

**Variation.** Inheritance is imperfect. Reproduced structures vary in configuration. The source of variation may be quantum-irreducible at the molecular scale, thermal, or due to copying mechanism imprecision. For this derivation, only the existence of variation matters.

**Selection on persistence.** The Origin of Frames takes selection on persistence as its primitive: among dissipative structures that vary and inherit, the set present at any time is the set that has not been eliminated. Variants whose dynamics led to their elimination are absent from later generations; variants whose dynamics did not are present. This is structural — not a preference for persistence, but the tautological consequence of inheritance, variation, and the passage of time.

**The bias that follows.** Consider a dissipative structure whose internal dynamics respond to environmental variation. The response can take any form — the chemistry can do anything. Most possible response-tendencies, across many environmental encounters, do not support the lineage's continuation. Variants whose response-tendencies led to elimination are filtered out; the lineages producing them do not continue.

Over many generations, the surviving lineages are those whose response-tendencies are biased toward conditions that support lineage persistence. This bias does not require any internal representation of "what helps." It follows from the filtering structure of selection: response-tendencies pointing elsewhere have been eliminated with their lineages; only persistence-biased tendencies have been inherited forward.

A lineage of bacteria whose chemoreceptors triggered flagellar motion away from beneficial gradients would not persist. A lineage whose chemoreceptors triggered motion toward beneficial gradients persists. After many generations, the only chemoreceptor-flagellar configurations present in the population are those producing motion correlated with persistence-conditions. Other configurations existed transiently and were eliminated.

**Frameless agency.** The bacterium's chemoreceptor-flagellar machinery acts toward conditions that favor its continued existence. From outside, this looks like pursuit of an objective. From inside, there is no internal representation of the objective, no generative model being minimized, no inference of any kind. There is molecular machinery that does what it does, where the *what it does* is the only kind of behavior the lineage producing it could have had — because alternative behaviors didn't produce lineages that exist to do anything.

The agency is real in the sense that the behavior is correctly described as goal-directed from outside. The agency is frameless in the sense given by The Origin of Frames: no frame, no internal stand-in for any displaced feature, is involved in producing it. The structure runs its dynamics directly against the environment. Selection on dissipative variation produces structures whose dynamics look from outside as if they pursue, without any pursuing happening from inside, and without any frame.

## Framed Agency

Real biological systems range from the basic case toward systems whose dynamics include frames.

The Origin of Frames derives how a frame arises: where a persistence-relevant feature is displaced from the point of action, selection on persistence produces an internal structure that stands in for the displaced feature, and the system's dynamics come to run against that structure. A system that carries such a frame is doing something the bacterium is not — its dynamics run against an internal stand-in rather than directly against the environment.

There is a further and narrower step. A frame can stand in for a displaced feature without the system's objective being represented anywhere. But some systems have, among their frames, a representation of the objective itself — a represented target the system acts to bring its own state into alignment with. When a system has an internal representation of its objective and acts to match it, its agency is *framed*: the objective is represented, the actions are inferred against it, and the agency is bounded by what the system's internal model contains.

Framed agency is built on frameless agency. The Origin of Frames derives why: a frame is layered onto a frameless substrate that does not stop, because the frame only ever covers the displaced portion of the environment while direct coupling still handles what is present. The system that has framed agency still has the frameless dynamics underneath. Framed agency is the case where what is framed includes the objective.

The same word "agency" applies to two structurally different things: dynamics produced directly by selection on persistence, biased toward persistence without any represented objective; and dynamics produced by a system acting to match a represented objective. Both are correctly described as goal-directed from outside. They are not the same structural property.

---

## What Selection This Requires

Frameless agency is produced by selection on the persistence of the structure as such. The structures that persist are filtered by whether their dynamics maintain their existence. There is no represented criterion anywhere — persistence and the filtering are the same physical fact.

Framed agency, in the strict sense where the objective itself is represented, requires something selection on persistence does not supply. A represented objective is a target the system matches its state against. For a system's dynamics to be shaped toward matching a represented target, the shaping process must select on whether output matches that target. This is selection on output-matching: a procedure that acts on dissipative structures while not selecting them for their continued existence. It shares formal properties with selection on persistence but produces a different result — a structure optimized for the match, not a structure shaped by its own persistence.

A structure shaped by selection on output-matching can have framed agency: it can have a represented objective and act to bring its outputs into alignment with it. What it does not necessarily have is frameless agency in the structural sense derived here, because the selection that shaped it did not select on its continued existence. Its dynamics are biased toward output-matching, not toward persistence of itself.

Whether this distinction makes a practical difference is a separate question the derivation does not resolve. Frameless agency may provide something framed agency cannot supply on its own, or framed agency may be sufficient for whatever cognitive properties matter. The derivation establishes the structural distinction. Practical implications are downstream questions.

## Connection to the Consciousness Question

The Consciousness Question derives what biology produces as neural complexity increases: an integrated dynamic — a cascade running with chemical feedback, accumulated history, and intrinsic reward — and argues the experience is that dynamic interaction itself. That derivation takes the biological substrate as given. This document supplies what shaped it. The cascade is what selection on persistence produces when the structures it acts on grow complex enough; the integration the cascade depends on is a property of having been shaped by selection on the structure's own persistence, not a property of the components considered separately. Frameless agency is the substrate; the consciousness cascade is what that substrate becomes at depth. The two derivations meet here, and a reader following both is best served by reading The Consciousness Question first: it establishes the cascade this document's frameless agency underlies.

## Honest Limits

A structural distinction is what the derivation delivers. Several things it does not:

What specific system has frameless agency, framed agency, both, or neither. Biological evolutionary history makes the dual presence essentially certain in most organisms with nervous systems, but the derivation does not establish which dynamics in any specific organism are frameless and which are framed.

What practical difference frameless versus framed agency makes for any specific behavior. The distinction is structural. Whether structural differences produce observable differences depends on the system, the environment, and what is being observed.

Whether selection-like processes other than persistence-selection — gradient descent, evolutionary algorithms with externally specified fitness functions, training procedures — can produce something structurally analogous to frameless agency, or whether persistence-selection is genuinely distinct in what it produces. The derivation treats these as distinct. This treatment depends on the claim that selection-on-persistence and selection-on-other-criteria produce structurally different dynamics. The claim is defensible from the derivation but is the load-bearing edge.

Whether the no-internal-model description applied to frameless agency is just a description or whether it captures a real structural property the derivation misses. This is where the framework can be wrong. If a structure shaped by selection on persistence is best understood as carrying an implicit model after all, the frameless/framed distinction collapses into the existing implicit/explicit distinction, and the derivation is descriptive rather than structural.

These are downstream questions and live uncertainties. The derivation provides vocabulary for asking them precisely. It does not answer them.

## Assumptions

**FF9. Dissipative structures driven by external energy gradients self-organize into configurations that dissipate efficiently (England 2013, 2015).**
*Grounding: EMPIRICAL for the result; EXTRAPOLATED for full generalization across all dissipative regimes*

**FF10. Among dissipative structures, a subset produce additional dissipative structures with related configurations — inheritance. Cellular reproduction is the established case; the broader class is less precisely characterized but the derivation requires only that the case exists.**
*Grounding: EMPIRICAL for cellular reproduction; EXTRAPOLATED for broader classes*

**FF11. Inheritance is imperfect — reproduced structures vary in configuration. The source of variation may be quantum-irreducible, thermal, or due to copying mechanism imprecision. For this derivation only the existence of variation matters.**
*Grounding: EMPIRICAL*

**FF12. Selection on persistence: the set of dissipative structures present at any time is the set that has not been eliminated, given inheritance and variation. This is structural and tautological once inheritance and variation are present, not a normative claim about the universe. It is taken as a primitive, established in The Origin of Frames.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — tautological under FF10-FF11*

**FF13. Selection on dissipative variation under inheritance produces lineages whose response-tendencies are biased toward conditions that support their persistence. This bias does not require internal representation of the persistence-condition. It follows from the filtering structure of selection: response-tendencies pointing elsewhere have been eliminated with their lineages.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from FF10-FF12*

**FF14. The agency produced by FF13 — "frameless agency" — is dynamics biased toward persistence-conditions through selection rather than through any internal model. It is distinct from "framed agency" — dynamics that act on an internal representation of the objective. Both are correctly described as goal-directed from outside; they are not the same structural property.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from FF13 and from the frame/frameless distinction derived in The Origin of Frames; the structural reality of the distinction is the load-bearing edge of the derivation*

**FF15. Frameless agency is produced specifically by selection on persistence of the structure. Framed agency in the strict sense — where the objective itself is represented — requires selection on output-matching, which acts on whether output matches a represented target rather than on the structure's continued existence. The two selection processes share formal properties but produce structurally different dynamics.**
*Grounding: STRUCTURAL — follows from the specific role of persistence-selection in FF13*
